Konstantin Tsiolkovsky

The father of spaceflight

Tsiolkovsky's life

  • 1857- Born in Izhevskoye in the Russian Empire to Polish immigrant parents.

  • 1867 - Became almost deaf due to scarlet fever.

  • 1870 - Not admitted to elementary school due to poor hearing.

    • Began self-teaching at home.
    • Became interested in science and math.
    • Began contemplating the possibility of space travel.

  • 1872 - Moved to Moscow to study at the Chertkovskaya Library.

  • 1876 - Passed exam to get a teacher's certificate.

    • About the same time, he built his first centrifuge to see what effects different levels of gravity would have on chickens.

  • 1880 - Moved to Borovsk to teach Math at a local school.

  • 1883 - Wrote academic paper "Free Space" (published 1956).

    • Outlined his ideas of the impact of space travel on Humanity.
    • Discussed effects of weightlessness and vacuum of space on space travelers.
    • Drew first scetches of space travelers in spacesuits.
    • And a spacecraft with gyroscopes for attitude control.

  • 1892 - His teaching position moved to Kaluga, the Russian Empire.

  • 1895 - Published "Dreams of Earth and Sky", which outlined the settlement of space, including asteroid mining and orbital greenhouses.

  • 1896 - Began studying the theory of jet propulsion.

  • 1903 - Published "Exploration of the World Space with Reaction Machines".

    • Derived "Tsiolkovsky's formula", a.k.a the "rocket equation".
    • This relates the changing mass of a rocket, its exhaust gas velocity, and its final speed.

  • 1929 - Published "The Space Rocket Trains", that detailed the need and use of multistage rockets to place large payloads into space.

  • 1935 - Died in Kaluga, USSR.

For more information about this titan of rocket science, please read about him on his Wikipedia Entry.
